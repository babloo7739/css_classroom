Iran Man

Iran is a country in Western Asia. It is bordered to the northwest by Armenia and Azerbaijan, to the north by the Caspian Sea, to the northeast by Turkmenistan, to the east by Afghanistan and Pakistan, to the south by the Persian Gulf and the Gulf of Oman, and to the west by Turkey and Iraq. The capital and largest city is Tehran. Iran is the second-largest country in the Middle East and the 17th-largest in the world. With over 83 million inhabitants, Iran is the world's 18th-most-populous country. The country is home to one of the world's oldest civilizations, dating back to the Proto-Elamite kingdom around 3200 BC. Iran is a founding member of the United Nations and a member of the Non-Aligned Movement, the Organization of Islamic Cooperation, and OPEC. The country's political system is a theocratic republic with a Supreme Leader as the highest authority. Iran has a diverse cultural heritage, with influences from various civilizations, including Persian, Islamic, and Western cultures. The official language is Persian (Farsi), and the majority of the population practices Shia Islam. Iran has a mixed economy, with significant contributions from the oil and gas sector, agriculture, and manufacturing. The country is known for its rich cultural traditions, including Persian literature, art, music, and cuisine.
